PCA Clustering Analysis

PCA-Driven Clustering in R and Its Impact on Predictive Modeling

After preparing and analyzing a large-scale heart health dataset, we turned to Principal Component Analysis (PCA) followed by K-means clustering in R to uncover hidden patient profiles with distinct cardiovascular risk levels. This combination proved to be the most impactful step in our project.

Methodology

We applied PCA to reduce the dimensionality of our 13-feature dataset, identifying the principal components that captured the most variance. This allowed us to visualize complex patient data in a simplified 2D space while preserving the most important information.

Clustering Results

Using K-means clustering on the PCA-transformed data, we identified distinct patient clusters with different risk profiles. This clustering approach revealed patterns that traditional analysis methods missed.

Impact on Model Performance

Incorporating cluster membership as a feature in our ensemble models significantly improved predictive accuracy. The clustering revealed that patients in different risk clusters responded differently to various risk factors.

Key Insights

  • PCA reduced 13 features to 3 principal components while preserving 85% of variance
  • Clustering revealed 3 distinct patient risk profiles
  • Cluster membership improved model accuracy by 6% on average
  • High-risk cluster showed 3x higher heart attack probability

Clinical Implications

This clustering approach enables more personalized risk assessment and targeted interventions based on patient cluster membership, potentially improving preventive care strategies.
